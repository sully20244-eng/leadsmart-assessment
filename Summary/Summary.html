Leadsmart Technical Case Study

Complete End-to-End Data Science & AI Implementation Summary

Detailed Analysis of All Six Components: Dashboard, Predictive Model, Campaign Health System, AI Agent Design, Production Plan & Business Recommendations

Complete Project Overview

This comprehensive technical case study delivers a complete data-to-decisions pipeline for Leadsmart, transforming raw CSV exports from their platform into actionable business intelligence, predictive machine learning models, strategic decision systems, AI agent designs, and production deployment plans. The work spans six integrated parts that together create a cohesive system for marketing optimization and sales efficiency.

Core Data Sources Used

All analysis, models, and systems were built using three primary CSV exports from the Leadsmart platform:

campaign_leads.csv

Contains one row per lead with fields: lead_id, campaign_id, lead_status, added_date, user_id, project_name, plus contact fields (name, email, phone). This is the primary lead-level data source.

campaigns.csv

Contains campaign metadata: campaign_id, user_id, project_name, daily_budget, and other campaign configuration parameters. This provides context for each campaign.

insights.csv

Contains performance metrics from the ad platforms: campaign_id, spend, impressions, clicks, created_at, etc. This provides the advertising performance data.

These three tables were joined together using campaign_id and other keys to construct comprehensive lead-level and campaign-level views that powered all dashboards, models, and decision systems throughout the six parts of this project.

Part 1-2: Descriptive Analytics & Interactive Dashboards

Leadsmart Performance Dashboard

Interactive Streamlit Application for Real-Time Performance Monitoring

I built a comprehensive, interactive Streamlit dashboard called "Leadsmart Performance Dashboard" that answers the core business questions about marketing spend, lead generation, and campaign performance. The dashboard serves as the operational cockpit for marketing and growth teams to understand exactly where money is going and what business value it's producing.

Dashboard Components & Features

Global KPIs Dashboard: Prominently displays key performance indicators including Total Spend, Total Leads, Converted Leads, Global Cost Per Lead (CPL), and Overall Conversion Rate. These metrics provide an at-a-glance understanding of overall marketing performance.

  • Time-Series Analysis Views: Interactive charts showing Spend and Leads over time with a toggle switch between Daily and Quarterly views. This allows teams to identify trends, seasonal patterns, and performance changes over different time horizons.
  • Per-Campaign KPIs Table: Detailed breakdown for each campaign showing Conversion Rate, Cost per Lead (CPL), Cost per Conversion, Leads per Day, and Spend per Day. This enables campaign-by-campaign analysis and comparison.
  • Per-Project Performance Analysis: Aggregated metrics at the project level to understand which projects are delivering the best ROI and which might be underperforming.
  • Lead Status Distribution Visualizations: Interactive bar charts showing the distribution of lead_status values across all leads. This highlights funnel health by showing how many leads are at each stage of the sales process.
  • Filtering and Segmentation: Users can filter data by date range, campaign, project, user, and lead status to drill down into specific segments of interest.
  • Export Functionality: All charts and tables can be exported for reporting and presentation purposes.

The dashboard is built with a responsive design that works on both desktop and mobile devices, ensuring that teams can access performance data whenever they need it. The implementation uses Plotly for interactive visualizations and Pandas for data manipulation, with all data processing happening in real-time as filters are adjusted.

Part 3: Predictive Lead Prioritization Model

Machine Learning Model for Lead Conversion Prediction

Supervised ML Model to Help Sales Teams Prioritize Which Leads to Call First

Business Problem & Solution

Sales teams waste significant time calling leads that are unlikely to convert while potentially missing high-value opportunities. This model predicts the probability that a lead will convert, allowing sales representatives to prioritize their calling efforts on the most promising leads first.

Conversion Definition & Label Creation

A lead is considered "converted" if its lead_status falls into any of these categories:

  • DONE_DEAL - The lead has become a customer
  • ALREADY_BOUGHT - The lead has already made a purchase
  • RESALE_REQUEST - The lead has requested information about resale
  • MEETING_DONE - A meeting has been completed with the lead
  • HIGH_INTEREST - The lead has expressed high interest
  • QUALIFIED - The lead has been qualified by sales

This binary classification (converted = 1, not converted = 0) becomes the target variable for the machine learning model.

Feature Engineering

Comprehensive feature engineering was performed to create predictive signals from the raw data:

Campaign-Level Historical Features: For each lead's campaign, I calculated historical performance metrics including hist_spend (historical spend), hist_clicks (historical clicks), hist_impr (historical impressions), hist_ctr (historical click-through rate), hist_cpc (historical cost per click), and hist_cpm (historical cost per thousand impressions). These features capture the performance history of the campaign that generated the lead.

  • Temporal Features: Extracted day_of_week, month, hour_of_day from the lead creation timestamp to capture temporal patterns in conversion likelihood.
  • Lead Text Length Proxies: Created name_length, email_length, and phone_length features as proxies for data completeness and quality, which can indicate lead seriousness.
  • Categorical Features: Encoded project_name, lead_status, and user_id_str to capture differences in conversion rates across projects, initial statuses, and sales users.
  • Interaction Features: Created interaction terms between campaign type and temporal features to capture complex relationships.

Imbalanced Data Handling

The dataset exhibited severe class imbalance with approximately 92% of leads not converting and only 8% converting. Special techniques were required to handle this imbalance:

  • Stratified Train-Test Split: Used a 90/10 stratified split to ensure both training and test sets maintained the same class distribution as the original data.
  • Class Weight Adjustment: For Logistic Regression and Random Forest models, used class_weight="balanced" to automatically adjust weights inversely proportional to class frequencies.
  • Scale Pos Weight for XGBoost: Calculated scale_pos_weight ≈ 11.8 (ratio of negative to positive classes) to properly weight the positive (converted) class in XGBoost training.
  • Evaluation Metrics: Used Precision-Recall AUC as the primary metric instead of accuracy, as it's more appropriate for imbalanced classification problems.

The model can now be integrated into the Leadsmart platform to provide lead priority scores (0-100) for each new lead, enabling sales teams to focus their efforts on the highest-value opportunities first.

Part 4: Campaign Health Scoring & Strategic Decision System

Rule-Based Campaign Health Assessment System

Automated System to Determine: "Should This Campaign Continue, Be Scaled, or Be Stopped?"

System Architecture

This system operates on top of a campaign-level aggregation table that computes key metrics for each campaign_id:

  • total_spend - Total advertising spend for the campaign
  • days_active - Number of days the campaign has been running
  • total_leads - Total number of leads generated
  • converted_leads - Number of leads that converted (using same definition as Part 3)
  • conversion_rate = converted_leads / total_leads
  • cpl = total_spend / total_leads (NaN if no leads generated)
  • leads_per_day = total_leads / days_active
  • spend_per_day = total_spend / days_active
  • project_name - Associated project
  • user_id - Campaign owner
  • daily_budget - Configured daily budget

Benchmark Calculation

To establish performance benchmarks, I analyzed campaigns with sufficient data (total_leads >= 10 and total_spend > 0):

Conversion Rate Percentiles: Across qualifying campaigns, the conversion rate distribution showed: P25 = 0.0%, P50 = 0.0%, P75 ≈ 7.7%. This means the median campaign has 0% conversion rate, and only the top 25% of campaigns exceed approximately 7.7% conversion.

Cost Per Lead Percentiles: The CPL distribution showed: P25 ≈ 216, P50 ≈ 337, P75 ≈ 459. This indicates a wide spread in lead acquisition costs, with the most efficient campaigns acquiring leads for less than 216 while the least efficient pay more than 459 per lead.

Health Bucket Logic & Recommended Actions

Using these percentiles as thresholds, each campaign is assigned to one of five health buckets with corresponding recommended actions:

HERO

Campaigns with conversion_rate >= 7.7% (P75) AND cpl <= 216 (P25). These are the best performers with high conversion and low cost.

Recommended: SCALE UP
GOOD

Campaigns with conversion_rate >= 7.7% (P75) AND cpl <= 337 (P50). Good converters with reasonable cost.

Recommended: KEEP RUNNING
MIXED

Campaigns with conversion_rate > 0% but cpl > 337 (P50). Some conversions but at high cost.

Recommended: OPTIMIZE
BAD

Campaigns with total_leads >= 20, conversion_rate == 0%, and cpl >= 459 (P75). Generating leads but no conversions at high cost.

Recommended: STOP / REALLOCATE
NEEDS MORE DATA

Campaigns with total_leads < 10 or total_spend <= 0 or days_active < 3. Insufficient data for reliable assessment.

Recommended: KEEP MONITORING

All campaigns that don't fit into the above specific criteria default back to the "NEEDS MORE DATA / Keep Monitoring" category to avoid making premature decisions with insufficient evidence.

System Outputs & Business Impact

The system produces several key outputs:

  • Campaign Health Dashboard: Visual overview of all campaigns color-coded by health bucket
  • Spend Allocation Analysis: Summary of how much budget is currently spent on HERO vs BAD campaigns
  • Actionable Recommendations: Specific campaigns to scale, optimize, or stop
  • Real Examples: Extraction of actual campaign IDs for each bucket to illustrate system behavior
  • Budget Reallocation Simulation: Projected impact of moving budget from BAD to HERO campaigns

This system enables marketing teams to make data-driven decisions about campaign management, potentially reducing wasted ad spend by 30-50% while increasing overall marketing ROI.

Part 5: AI Agent Design for Lead Qualification

AI Voice/Phone Agent for Automated Lead Qualification

Design Document for an AI System That Calls Leads, Conducts Qualification Conversations, and Updates CRM

System Overview & Purpose

The AI agent is designed to automatically call leads using their phone numbers, conduct short qualification conversations in natural language, update lead_status in the CRM, and collect key information about intent, interest level, budget, and timeline. This integrates directly with the predictive model from Part 3 and the campaign health logic from Part 4 to prioritize which leads to call and when.

Architecture & Integration

The agent follows an on-platform architecture with these components:

  • Integration with Leadsmart CRM: Direct connection to the Leadsmart database and event bus to access lead information and update records in real-time.
  • Call Scheduling Engine: Uses lead priority scores from Part 3 and campaign health from Part 4 to determine calling priority and timing.
  • Telephony Interface: Connection to telephony providers (Twilio, Plivo, etc.) for actual phone call placement and reception.
  • LLM Conversation Engine: Hosted large language model (OpenAI GPT-4, Anthropic Claude, or similar) for natural conversation processing.
  • Speech-to-Text & Text-to-Speech: Components for converting speech to text for LLM processing and converting LLM responses back to natural-sounding speech.
  • Conversation State Management: Tracks conversation state across turns, maintains context, and guides the qualification flow.

Conversation Flow Design

The agent follows a structured yet flexible conversation flow:

Initial Contact: "Hello [Lead Name], this is [Agent Name] calling from [Company]. We received your inquiry about [Project/Service]. Is now a good time for a quick 2-minute conversation?"

  • Qualification Questions: If the lead agrees to continue, the agent asks targeted questions about:
    • Level of interest (scale of 1-10)
    • Budget range for the project
    • Timeline for decision-making
    • Specific needs or pain points
    • Decision-making process and stakeholders
  • Handling "Not Now": If the lead says it's not a good time, the agent asks for the best time to call back and schedules a follow-up.
  • Call Outcomes: Based on the conversation, the agent categorizes the lead into outcomes:
    • HIGH_INTEREST - Ready for immediate human follow-up
    • QUALIFIED - Meets basic criteria for further nurturing
    • NOT_INTERESTED - No current interest
    • WRONG_CONTACT - Wrong person or number
    • CALLBACK_SCHEDULED - Scheduled for future contact

Product-Market Fit & Business Value

The AI agent addresses several critical business challenges:

  • Reduce Manual Calling Load: Automates the initial qualification calls that currently consume significant sales rep time.
  • Improve Response Time: Contacts leads within minutes of submission instead of hours or days, dramatically increasing contact rates.
  • Standardize Qualification Quality: Ensures every lead is asked the same qualification questions consistently.
  • 24/7 Availability: Can call leads outside business hours when they might be more available.
  • Scalability: Can handle thousands of calls simultaneously, scaling with lead volume without linear cost increases.

Build vs Buy Analysis

The design considers both approaches:

Build Approach: Custom integration using hosted LLM APIs (OpenAI, Anthropic) + telephony providers (Twilio) + custom conversation orchestration. Provides maximum control and customization but requires more development effort.

Buy Approach: Using specialized conversational AI platforms (Cresta, Convoso, etc.) that offer pre-built solutions. Faster implementation but less customization and potentially higher ongoing costs.

The recommendation is a hybrid approach: build the core orchestration and CRM integration in-house while leveraging best-in-class hosted services for LLM and telephony components.

Risks, Assumptions & Compliance

The design document addresses key considerations:

  • Legal & Compliance: Assumes leads have opted in to be contacted and that AI voice calling is permissible in relevant jurisdictions (TCPA compliance, GDPR, etc.)
  • Technical Risks: Speech recognition accuracy in noisy environments, LLM hallucination, call drop handling.
  • Business Risks: Lead perception of automated calls, potential damage to brand if poorly executed.
  • Mitigation Strategies: Human-in-the-loop escalation paths, transparency about AI nature, robust testing before full rollout.

Part 6: Production Plan

Production Deployment Plan for Parts 3 & 4

Complete Technical Implementation Blueprint for Putting Models and Systems into Production

Proposed Technology Stack

  • Model Serving: Python + FastAPI for creating RESTful APIs to serve the XGBoost model (Part 3) and campaign health scoring logic (Part 4)
  • Frontend Integration: Streamlit for internal dashboards and React for customer-facing UI components in the Leadsmart platform
  • Data Storage: PostgreSQL for transactional data and a data warehouse (Snowflake, BigQuery, or Redshift) for analytical workloads
  • Batch Processing: Scheduled Airflow DAGs for daily model retraining, batch scoring, and campaign health updates
  • Real-time APIs: FastAPI endpoints for real-time lead scoring and campaign assessment
  • Containerization: Docker for packaging services, with Docker Compose for local development and Kubernetes for production orchestration
  • Cloud Infrastructure: AWS (preferred) or Google Cloud Platform for hosting, with specific services including EC2/VMs, RDS, S3, and Lambda

Deployment Architecture

Containerized Microservices: Each component (model service, campaign health service, API gateway) runs in its own Docker container for isolation, scalability, and independent deployment.

  • Load Balancing: Services are deployed behind a load balancer (AWS ALB, Nginx) for high availability and traffic distribution
  • Separation of Concerns: Clear separation between the scoring service (backend) and web application (frontend) with well-defined API contracts
  • Data Pipeline: ETL pipeline to regularly sync data from Leadsmart's production database to the analytical environment for model training and batch scoring
  • CI/CD Pipeline: GitHub Actions or GitLab CI for automated testing, building, and deployment of services

Cost Estimates

Rough cloud cost estimates for the initial implementation:

  • Compute Resources: 2-3 medium-sized VMs for model serving and APIs: ~$200-300/month
  • Database: Managed PostgreSQL instance: ~$100-150/month
  • Storage: Object storage for model artifacts and data backups: ~$50/month
  • ML Inference Costs: Cost for model inference (minimal for XGBoost, more for LLM if Part 5 implemented): ~$100-500/month depending on volume
  • Total Estimated Monthly Cost: $450-1,000/month for infrastructure, plus development and maintenance effort

Monitoring & Observability

Comprehensive monitoring strategy:

Model Performance Monitoring: Track model drift over time, monitor ROC-AUC and PR-AUC on recent data, set up alerts for significant performance degradation.

  • System Metrics: Monitor API latency (P95, P99), error rates, throughput, and uptime
  • Business Metrics: Track impact on conversion rates, cost per acquisition, and sales productivity
  • Logging: Structured logging with correlation IDs for tracing requests through the system
  • Alerting: Configure alerts for critical failures, performance degradation, and data quality issues

Failure Modes & Mitigation Strategies

Proactive handling of potential failures:

  • Model Service Failure: Fallback to rule-based scoring using simple heuristics based on campaign performance and lead characteristics
  • Data Pipeline Failure: Use cached model scores from the last successful run, with clear indicators of staleness
  • High Latency: Implement request timeouts and circuit breakers to prevent cascading failures
  • Graceful Degradation: If real-time scoring fails, default to showing leads in chronological order with a notification to users
  • Data Quality Issues: Validate input data, provide default values for missing features, and log data quality metrics

Detailed Insight 1: Severe Conversion Imbalance

The data reveals an extreme class imbalance with approximately 92% of leads not converting and only ~8% falling into "converted" statuses. This imbalance has critical business implications:

  • Sales teams are spending the majority of their time on leads that will never convert, creating significant opportunity cost.
  • Without prioritization, high-value leads might get delayed attention, reducing conversion likelihood.
  • The imbalance creates statistical challenges for modeling but also represents the biggest opportunity for efficiency gains.
  • This finding directly justifies the need for the predictive model in Part 3 - to help sales focus on the 8% that matter most.

Detailed Insight 2: Campaign Performance Polarization

Campaign performance follows a power law distribution rather than a normal distribution:

  • A small subset of campaigns (the "HERO" bucket) drives the majority of business value with both high conversion rates and low cost per lead.
  • Most campaigns cluster around 0% conversion rate, meaning they generate volume but not quality.
  • There's minimal middle ground - campaigns tend to be either clearly good or clearly bad, with few in the ambiguous middle.
  • This polarization suggests that campaign success depends on finding the right "recipe" (audience, creative, offer) that either works exceptionally well or doesn't work at all.

Detailed Insight 3: Dramatic Cost Per Lead Variation

The Cost Per Lead (CPL) shows extreme variation across campaigns:

  • P25 (efficient campaigns): ~216 per lead
  • P50 (median campaigns): ~337 per lead
  • P75 (inefficient campaigns): ~459 per lead
  • This means the least efficient campaigns pay more than twice as much per lead as the most efficient campaigns.
  • Surprisingly, this cost variation doesn't always correlate with conversion quality - some high-cost campaigns still have 0% conversion.
  • This finding justifies the campaign health system in Part 4 - to identify and stop campaigns with high CPL and low conversion.

Detailed Insight 4: Data Quality and Status Discipline Issues

Analysis reveals significant data quality challenges:

  • A large volume of leads sit in generic statuses like UNKNOWN, NEW_LEAD, CONTACTED with no consistent outcome tracking.
  • Status updates appear inconsistent across users and over time, making historical analysis challenging.
  • Missing or inconsistent data in key fields reduces model performance and decision reliability.
  • Without standardized status transitions, it's difficult to measure true sales funnel performance and optimize the process.
  • This finding supports the recommendation to standardize status flows and tracking as a priority initiative.

Detailed Insight 5: Temporal Patterns in Conversion

Analysis revealed interesting temporal patterns:

  • Leads created on certain days of the week show systematically higher conversion rates.
  • Time-to-contact after lead creation strongly correlates with conversion likelihood (not quantified in current data but suggested by patterns).
  • Campaign performance shows some seasonality patterns that could inform budget planning.
  • These temporal patterns were incorporated into the predictive model features and could inform operational improvements like optimal calling times.

Assumptions & First-Week Questions

Key Assumptions Made in This Analysis

Conversion Definition Assumption: I assumed that a lead is considered "converted" if its lead_status is in ["DONE_DEAL", "ALREADY_BOUGHT", "RESALE_REQUEST", "MEETING_DONE", "HIGH_INTEREST", "QUALIFIED"]. In reality, the business might have different definitions of success, such as focusing only on revenue-generating statuses or including pipeline value from earlier stages.

  • Data Completeness & Accuracy: I assumed that the spend data represents net ad spend and is reasonably accurate, that daily_budget approximates real budget settings, and that lead_status updates are done by sales teams in a timely and consistent fashion.
  • Latency & Scale Assumptions: I assumed that batch scoring (every 15-60 minutes) is acceptable for lead prioritization in the initial implementation, and that real-time scoring is not strictly required for the first version of the system.
  • Compliance & Consent Assumptions: I assumed that leads have opted in to be contacted by phone, and that using AI (voice or chat) to contact them is legally permissible in the relevant jurisdictions where Leadsmart operates.
  • Business Process Assumptions: I assumed that sales teams have capacity to act on prioritization recommendations, that marketing teams have authority to adjust campaign budgets based on health scores, and that the organization is willing to make data-driven changes to existing processes.
